Pojects

 

 

Partial Binarization of Generative Adversarial Networks

 

During 2017 summer, I worked on a summer research project in Notre Dame University. The work explored the binarization of deep convolutional generative adversarial networks (DCGANs) for memory reduction and speedup. It is called partial binarization of generative adversarial networks(PBGAN).There is great demand to run neural networks on all kinds of power-sensitive devices like wearable devices and unmanned vehicles. The paper has been submitted to 2018 AAAI Conference and I am the second author.

 

Eye tracking project

 

I am currently doing research of an eye tracking project in the lab at USTC. This project's goal is to build a synthetic system based on eye tracking and its application. We use infrared camera to capture the movement of human's eyes. Then, a helmet is used to measure the posture of the tester's head so that we can use computer to figure out what the tester is actually looking at. We use machine learning methods for iris image localization.

Mendocino Motor

In my second year, I build a Mendocino motor to attend the campus EM competition using 3D printer and solar panel. The whole rotor can levitate in the air with the help of the strong permanent magnets. It rotates smoothly under the sun.
